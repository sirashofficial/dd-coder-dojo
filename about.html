Our Purpose

Our Mission

We believe that every child deserves access to quality technology education. DD (Digital Democracy) Coder Dojo provides free coding classes every weekday (Mon-Fri) for ages 7-17, teaching building games, apps & animations using Scratch, Python, & Web Development, while fostering problem-solving & creative thinking.

Our Approach

From blocks to big ideas, we guide students through fun projects with peer mentors in a supportive community environment. Located at 178 Austerville Drive, Wentworth, Durban, we're supported by KCA (Keep A Child Alive) in partnership with the Raspberry Pi Foundation.

Our Journey

2022

Laying the Foundation

The first informal sessions began with a focus on digital literacy and fun with Scratch and typing. Early workshops revealed strong interest from kids, parents, and local schools in our community.

2023

Growth and Structure

We officially adopted the Coder Dojo model and began holding weekly sessions. Introduced Code.org and Scratch as our primary teaching tools, and created a mentor system to help older learners guide younger ones.

2024

Innovation and Community

Expanded our program to include themed events like Coolest Projects Showcase, Family Movie Night, and Christmas Celebrations. Introduced micro:bits to intermediate and senior students, and started incorporating typing lessons for juniors.

2025

Empowering Through Experience

Launched a year-long structured curriculum using Code.org and micro:bits. Started the Hackathon Fundraiser to boost skills and raise funds, with increased parental engagement through WhatsApp updates and "Did You Know?" tech facts.
