Our Purpose

Our Mission

We believe that every child deserves access to quality technology education. DD (Digital Democracy) Coder Dojo provides free coding classes every weekday (Mon-Fri) for ages 7-17, teaching building games, apps & animations using Scratch, Python, & Web Development, while fostering problem-solving & creative thinking.

Our Approach

From blocks to big ideas, we guide students through fun projects with peer mentors in a supportive community environment. Located at 178 Austerville Drive, Wentworth, Durban, we're supported by KCA (Keep A Child Alive) in partnership with the Raspberry Pi Foundation.

For Parents

Supporting your child on their coding journey

How You Can Support Your Child

  • Encourage regular attendance and celebrate progress
  • Ask your child to show you their projects
  • Help them find time for practice at home
  • Stay connected via our WhatsApp group for updates

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know coding to help my child?
No! Our mentors guide all students. Your encouragement is what matters most.
Is there homework?
There's no formal homework, but practicing at home is encouraged.
How do I stay informed?
Join our WhatsApp group and check our website for updates and resources.

Learning Outcomes & Skill Progression

Clear goals and milestones in each of our programs

🧩

Junior Ninjas (7-10)

By the end of this program, your child will:

  • Understand basic coding concepts using Scratch
  • Create simple games and animations
  • Work collaboratively and think creatively
Pathway: Scratch → Teamwork → Creativity
🐍

Code Explorers (11-13)

By the end of this program, your child will:

  • Write code in Python and build web pages
  • Solve real-world problems with technology
  • Develop logical thinking and resilience
Pathway: Python & Web → Problem Solving → Independence
💡

Tech Innovators (14-17)

By the end of this program, your child will:

  • Build advanced projects in Python & JavaScript
  • Experiment with hardware (e.g., Micro:bit)
  • Mentor others and develop leadership skills
Pathway: Advanced Coding → Hardware → Leadership
