Learn to Code!

Learning to code is about creating, building confidence, gaining skills, and having fun. Here are our favorite resources to help you on your coding journey!

Why Learn to Code?

Learning to code is about so many things: creating, learning computational and analytical thinking, understanding the power of computing, telling your stories, building confidence, gaining skills, and having fun. There are so many resources and tutorials out there to get started. Here are a few of our favorites used in DD Coder Dojo!

Beginner Resources

Perfect for getting started - no experience needed! These resources teach the logic of how computers think.

  • CS Unplugged

    You don't need a computer to learn the logic of how computers think!

  • Blockly Games

    Fun games that teach drag and drop coding (online or download offline).

  • Scratch

    Our platform of choice! Create simple programs or complex games and interactive projects.

  • Google CS First

    A curriculum including videos and activities all using Scratch.

Physical Computing

Bring your code to life with physical devices and interactive projects!

  • BBC micro:bit

    Affordable microcontroller boards - code with MakeCode blocks, JavaScript or Python.

  • MakeCode

    Can interact with other devices and programs including Minecraft!

  • MakeyMakey

    Turn everyday objects into touchpads and combine with Scratch projects.

  • Raspberry Pi

    An affordable computer (~R1000) for robotics, automation, and so much more!

More Advanced

Ready for the next level? These tools help you build real apps and complex programs.

  • Snap!

    Based on Scratch, Snap! allows more complex coding, particularly with data.

  • MIT App Inventor

    Another great MIT creation - build real mobile apps with drag-and-drop!

  • freeCodeCamp

    Comprehensive web development curriculum with certificates.

  • Codecademy

    Interactive lessons in Python, JavaScript, HTML/CSS and more.

Global Movements

Join international movements bringing coding education to everyone!

  • Africa Code Week

    International movement to bring coding to Africa - tons of links and resources!

  • Hour of Code

    Global movement reaching tens of millions of students in 180+ countries.

  • Code.org

    Our primary curriculum partner - courses for all ages and skill levels.

For Parents & Mentors

Resources to help parents and mentors support young coders on their journey.

  • Common Sense Media: Coding for Kids

    Guide for parents on the benefits of coding education.

  • Raspberry Pi Teaching Resources

    Classroom resources for teaching with Raspberry Pi.

  • Scratch Ideas & Help

    Video tutorials and project ideas for Scratch - some available in isiZulu!

Interactive Tools

Online tools and platforms that make learning to code more interactive and engaging.

  • Replit

    Browser-based coding environment for multiple languages.

  • CodePen

    Online community for testing and showcasing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

  • Trinket

    Browser-based Python and HTML environments for education.

Our Key Partners

Code.org

Block Coding Computer Science K-12 Education

Code.org is a non-profit dedicated to expanding access to computer science in schools. Their curriculum forms the backbone of our programs, providing structured learning paths for students of all ages.

We use their Hour of Code activities, foundational courses for beginners, and CS Discoveries for our intermediate students. These resources are professionally designed with inclusivity in mind.

Elementary Courses Middle School Courses Hour of Code

Raspberry Pi Foundation

Physical Computing Python micro:bit

The Raspberry Pi Foundation creates computing education resources used worldwide. Their project-based learning approach helps our students apply coding skills to real-world problems and physical computing projects.

We integrate their project resources into our curriculum, helping students progress from basic to advanced programming concepts through hands-on projects with micro:bits and Raspberry Pi computers.

MIT Scratch

Creative Coding Storytelling isiZulu Support

Scratch is our platform of choice for the creativity it allows learners. Students can create simple programs or complex games and interactive projects. We've even contributed to translating Scratch 3.0 to isiZulu!

Extended with MakeyMakey and BBC micro:bit microcontroller boards, Scratch projects come to life through physical computing, making coding tangible and exciting for our students.

About These Resources

The resources linked on this page are created and maintained by their respective organizations. DD (Digital Democracy) Coder Dojo provides these links as a service to our community and does not claim ownership of the materials.

We carefully curate these resources to ensure they align with our educational approach and values. All resources are free to access, though some may offer premium features not required for our curriculum.

There are so many resources and tutorials out there to get started - search on YouTube, Google, social media etc. These are just a few of our favorites. What are yours? Let us know and stay up to date with our work!

When using these resources, please respect their terms of service and copyright notices. If you discover any broken links or have suggestions for additional resources, please contact us at ashley@kcasa.org.za.
